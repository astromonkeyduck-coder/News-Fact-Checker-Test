Why Digital Literacy Matters

In today's digital age, misinformation spreads faster than ever. Learning to distinguish fact from fiction is crucial for making informed decisions and protecting yourself from manipulation.

üîç Fact-Checking Strategies

  • Check Multiple Sources: Don't rely on just one source
  • Verify the Source: Look for official websites and reputable organizations
  • Look for Evidence: Factual news provides citations and evidence
  • Question Sensational Claims: If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is
  • Check the Date: Old news can be misleading when shared as current

üö® Red Flags for Misinformation

  • Claims of "miracle cures" or "secret knowledge"
  • Sources with suspicious names or no credibility
  • Emotional language designed to provoke outrage
  • Conspiracy theories without evidence
  • Get-rich-quick schemes or too-good-to-be-true offers

‚úÖ Reliable Sources

  • Government websites (.gov domains)
  • Peer-reviewed scientific journals
  • Established news organizations
  • Official company press releases
  • Academic institutions and research centers
